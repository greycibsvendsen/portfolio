DRIP, DRIP Coffee App

Study Case

The App DRIP, DRIP is helping to gain an understand to finding out where the best coffee and coffee beans is and find what the best way to there that can teach the best ways to make coffee.
Provide the best up to date knowledge base of making good coffee and when is the optimal time for healthy consumption. Have the largest collection high end coffee roasting companies who produce exceptional quality beans and coffee drinks.

Discover

Discovery of Problem

A lot of people love great coffee whether they are finding it or making their own special brew. The coffee culture has become bigger over the years as internationalization of coffee beans increases and warmer climates persist. We drink coffee more than ever before. As more coffee lovers enter into the craze, it’s getting harder and harder to find the right place to grab a cup. There are 100s of coffee places and cafes all over the city, but there needs to be a guide to find the best coffee and if you choose, to make your own.

The Project Goal

Have the largest collection of high end coffee roasting suppliers

Provide the most up-to-date information of making good coffee

Provide educational content of the proper way to brew a cup

Research

Survey Results:

I created a short survey of various coffee personas consist of newcomers to coffee, diehard fans, and organic coffee makers . Each persona enjoyed coffee slightly differently. I found that they wanted different experiences depending on what they found important. I received 10 responses but believe If I had more, I would have gained more insight to the various needs and user requirements. I found that except for the newcomers, each persona almost entirely drink coffee everyday and go out at least twice a week. The Apple's iPhone was the dominant phone and when asked what they wanted out of a coffee app, they responded with: wanting the ability for coffee to be delivered quickly and conveniently. When reviewing coffee on the app, they wanted something educational and also support for local stores and organic coffee.

Conclusions

I found that there is a high demand for coffee apps even though there is very heavy competition. No app seems to do it all and more testing would be able to surface what users truly want. There is also an element that I was not able to explore with supporting local businesses. Coffee suppliers and businesses that make coffee could benefits from marketing through a established app like this.

For this project, the design and development became a success for users and they generally received what they were looking for.
